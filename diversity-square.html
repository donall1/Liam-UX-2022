Web and Mobile

Diversity Square Media

I was the Lead UX Designer for the website and the marketing collateral for
Diversity Square Media Company. Specializing in the publication of books
and movies, Diversity Square was created in 1997 and did not have an online
presence. This project was inherently created to put Diversity Square on the
map.

CHALLENGE

GOAL

Diversity Square had absolutely Zero of an online presence,
their branding identity was recently updated and they
needed marketing collateral and most importantly, a
website to connect all their products and give life
to their online presence.

DUTIES

Role

My role in this position was to create an entire
website from the ground up with little design
feedback and do it in a tight time frame. Since our
CEO was unfamiliar with websites and the process, and
me being the only designer on the team, keeping
organized and attention to detail was of the upmost
importance.

Part One

Data Analytics

Analytics and Data Review

Initial data collection for Diversity Square's
website was challenging due to the lack of an
online presence. So for this project, we cross
referenced many different competitors and
directed the flow of our product from the data
compiled.

Enhanced User Feedback

We conducted a larger test from the data
compiled and began speaking with users to
begin narrowing down what specifically needed
to be on the site, and what people were looking
to see from Diversity Square in the future.

UI Concept Test

With the data compiled and the general content
of the product gathered, I began the design of
the UI from the ground up, going through process
after process. The initial concept that I had was
to create an Ajax website that navigates like an
app, with transitions moving in a swiping motion
and no navigation bar, with everything being
accessible within a few clicks.

Part Two

Design and Implementation

Design by Development

This website was intentionally built to stand out
from the competition with a memorable UI that
can be accessed like an app. With that design
concept in mind, we had to simultaneously
develop the app in code as we were designing it
in order to make sure that the site was going to
function properly.

Iterative UI Design

User testing needed to be in the forefront of this project due to the unusual UI concept that we were trying to implement. At every stage of the process there was at least one user or QA tester accessing the site to ensure functionality.

Keeping The Design Simple

As the process of designing and developing went
along, organization was very important to make
sure that changes were being made properly. A
simple, Ajax accessible design was the basis of
the whole project and keeping the original
design in mind was imperative.

Part Three

Testing and Performance

Technical Blunder

As the development process went along, more
and more quality control issues began to pile up.
This design doesn't have a lot of inspiration or
community to assist with issues. Unfortunately,
as we were finalizing the site the bugs and the
crashing was too much to launch as a final
product, and I had to make the inherent but
logical decision to completely redesign the
product.

Complete Website Redesign

Unfortunately, the product I set out to create was too ambitious and it didn't fit
what Diversity Square is trying accomplish with this product. I had to backtrack
all the way to the beginning and redefine what the product's goals were and
what the USER was going to accomplish by using our product.

New Product Design

I redefined our goals as a company and reset the
project back to square 1. This time I focused on a
site that was easily accessible by everyone. The
new UI was meant to be easily navigable and I
actually cut the number of initial pages in half by
refocusing content and restructuring the site
from top to bottom.

New QA Testing and Goals

We also changed what the goals of the QA
testing were, changing from a memorable site to
one that loaded quickly and was easily
navigated by users of all levels of technical
expertise. Our new product was much more
accessible and it conveyed exactly what
Diversity Square is and why you should care.
Along with that, we also used heatmaps to make
sure that there weren't any unusual outliers that
would detract from the accessibility of the product.

Part Four

Final Designs

Final Thoughts

In the end this project turned out great, but the process did take a while to get down. The biggest challenge was that we had to go all the way back to the drawing board. Which can be frustrating, but we all kept our composure and continued the grind and in the end we delivered a fitting product that Diversity Square can now build on for the future.
