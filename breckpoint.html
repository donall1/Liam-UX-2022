Web and Mobile

Breckpoint Insurance

I designed and managed the website for Breckpoint Insurance Company,
a Las Vegas based Self-Insurance company that specializes in the
administration and implementation of Risk Management Services,
Limited Benefits, and the creation and management of Captive Insurance.

CHALLENGE

GOAL

Breckpoints previous website had become a little buggy and
outdated. It also didn't do a good job of explaining what
we did and why we were beneficial to employers looking to
save money on their employee benefits. The challenge was
trying to simplify our content and our purpose to those who
don't understand insurance, and display it in a manner that
represents our brand identity.

DUTIES

Role

My role was to design the structural UI page by page
and properly display every product that we offer, while
also making the website simple an accessible to future
clients and brokers looking to see what we offer and
why they should choose us.

Part One

Concept and Troubleshoot

Analytics and Data Review

While Breckpoint was established officially in
2017, they have been under several other names
(EON Blue, Freedomcare, etc.) since 2003.
However, their 2017 website was the only online
presence they have had. While data collection is
not as large, the ability to specifically focus on
one asset and troubleshoot it sped up the
process for the design and UI of the website.

The Rainbow Spreadsheet

After our larger user test, we used a more
focused user test to determine the actual causes
of our low site retention. We chose 5 individual
users who never used the site before and asked
them to navigate the site like normal. Our data
indicated that the site was too overwhelming of
information, and the navigation was frustrating.

UX Overhaul Test

We also discovered that our site was buggy on
almost every page. The loading times were way
too slow and that was the biggest factor in users
not staying on the site. Putting the site on a
heavy load would cause it to crash, therefore
keeping the new site as clean coded and running
smoothly was the #1 priority, especially as the
new site would have triple the number of pages
as the previous.

Part Two

Design and Implementation

Initial Design

Breckpoint has a unique brand identity, with 13
different colors making up its color theme. With
each product encompassing one or multiple
colors to help it stand out from the others. This
was a unique challenge in todays modern world,
as designs are becoming more and more
simplistic, this rainbow theme would need to
embody the brand and make it look like the
current decade.

Mockups Plus a Big Blunder

Our initial sketches were then translated into our first mockups, in just 30 days the
entire sitemap was mocked up page by page and we were ready to move on to the
actual implementation to see if it worked. However, our initial mockups were being
created in Adobe Photoshop, and not Adobe XD or proper prototyping software. This
caused major headaches for our development team in terms of responsiveness and
the scalability of the site. At this point I took over the UI design of the site and
converted every page to XD one by one. This would enable much quicker
development time and the flexibility to create new assets and try new things.

Final Designs and Development

As our designs started to come into fruition, we
worked closely with our development team to
bring these designs to life in the form of code.
Since our site was so large, we didn't wait for
every page to be finalized, as pages were
approved development and design was
happening simultaneously.

Part Three

Testing and Performance

Final Code and KPI Testing

As code was being finalized we continued more
user testing to keep our goals and our original
problems in check. We used Google Analytics to
check the performance and accessibility of our
site to make sure the designs were matching our
initial concept and made adjustments as needed.

Heat Mapping

We used heat maps in order to test the
navigation, which was one of our biggest issues
with the original site, through our continued
process page after page we were able to funnel
the user through our navigation without
compromising our original design system.
Throughout the finishing stages of the site we
continued heat mapping each page to make sure
the viewer wasn't going off course.

Part Four

Final Designs

Final Thoughts

This project was very enlightening because of the big mistake that led to huge backtracking and redesign. But our team worked well under the pressure and the adjustments we made were astronomical in achieving our original goals for this product. In the end I am very happy with the way the project turned out.
