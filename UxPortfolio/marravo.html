E-Commerce

Marravo Fitness Camp

I was responsible for the Website UI back in 2016 when Marravo first started
in the E-commerce market. In 2020, I returned and took on the Lead UX
Designer Position to modernize and redesign their entire product line. map.

CHALLENGE

GOAL

Marravo already had a previous product line that was
outdated, and a website that was functional but visually
needed improvements and a better sales funnel. Since I was
the one who designed the previous UI, the biggest
challenge was staying true to the company's interests while
also completing a complete redesign in a very short
timeframe.

DUTIES

Role

My role was to create a new and improved sales funnel
in order to create more sales, while also designing a
new and improved UI that was up to date with current
visual standards.

Part One

Data Collection

Analytics and Data Review

Marravo has used Shopify as their platform since
2016, so there was plenty of data to go back on to
see what was working and what wasn't. The
general consensus was that a new sales funnel was
needed in order to drive sales of the new product
line. The analysis of the data was a huge indicator
of what specifically needed to be targeted.

The Rainbow Spreadsheet

We conducted a user test on 5 individuals who
navigated the site and they identified the
problems that would lead to drop-off before a sale
was completed. A lot of the site was automated by
Shopify, so the UI and the speed of the site wasn't
an issue, therefore, we had to think outside the
box in terms of identifying problems that would
direct where the site needed to be addressed.

UX Testing and Checkout

Many of user tests identified that the site was easy
to understand and navigate, but the process of
checkout and shipping was too long. In today's
day and age, people want to check out products
online very quick. Conglomerates like Amazon
have pioneered how fast online shopping is, and
our store would need to be just as fast if we
wanted to drive more sales.

Part Two

Initial Design and Funnel

Initial Design

Our branding guide was updated shortly before
the start of this project, so the initial concept and
the design of the site was quick, I sketched up a
few mockups and was able to get the overall
navigation of the site down through our new and
improved sales funnel. Looking at the old site was
a huge help, being the one who designed it I could
quickly identify issues and fixes which sped up this
process.

Flexibility Through Design

What was unique about this product was that most of images were not In place, as we had models doing photoshoots of the new product line as we were in the initial design phase. We wouldn't know the sizes, the structure, what the images would look like and how they would compliment (or not) the website. Our design had to be incredibly flexible and change on the fly as images were coming in, because the timeline for this project was so tight.

Part Three

Performance Testing

Shopify Speed Testing

This new site was much larger than the previous
one, and while we were building our platform
on Shopify, that did a lot of the optimization for us,
its not perfect. The times for loading and
converting sales was very small, so we had to do
extra performance testing to make sure that our
speeds were within the TTS (Time to Sale).

Final Designs and Heat Mapping

As the page by page was being completed we were
able to move to our final stages, testing if the
sales funnel was working the way it was supposed
to. We did this though the use of Heat Mapping.
This was the bread and butter of our testing
phases, as we were able to see exactly where users
were going and could refine our navigation to a T.

Part Four

Final Designs

Final Thoughts

This project turned out very clean and the sales of the site increased over 20% just within the first month of the new sites new launch. The conversion of our sales funnel and the new navigation of the site came together beautifully and while the process wasn't completely smooth sailing, we were able to keep the project neat and organized and the project turned out very well.
